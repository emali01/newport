Paints

In order to reduce Titanium dioxide (TiO2), it is highly recommended to formulate a mix using Calcium Carbonate. Titanium dioxide (TiO2) is very popular due to its high refractive index and is hence widely used in the paint industry as a white pigment. As Titanium dioxide forms the major cost in paint manufacturing, it is generally substituted by some other industrial mineral fillers such as Calcium Carbonate. We make a special grade to suit the paint and pigments market.  

  • White Calcium carbonate

    • Often industry specialists will argue that the brightness of Calcium Carbonate determines the usefulness in the paint industry. A brilliant white background colour is only achieved by selecting the very best raw materials, as well as constant quality assurance processes.

  • Ultra-fine Calcium Carbonate

    • Ultra-fine Calcium Carbonate particle size is essential for successful paint & coating formulation. With a small uniform ideal distribution of particle size and high whiteness, Calcium Carbonate enhances the process-ability features of paint. It is an anti-setting mediator and an opacity agent that enhances the stability and gloss features of the paint. It also functions as an anti-rust agent.

  • Extender in Paints

    • Calcium Carbonate is used as an extender in the paints industry. It is a relatively soft, insoluble mineral that is widely used to reduce cost and to control shrinkage in adhesive formulations. Fine Calcium Carbonate can maintain viscosity in paints as well as improve physical strength and replace other more-expensive ingredients, improve gap-filling properties and modify viscosity.

Coatings

Coatings quality are greatly affected by the substitution of Titanium dioxide (TiO2), which affects the quality of coatings in terms of stability, coverage (opacity), brightness (gloss), scrub resistance (film toughness). Obtaining the right quality of Calcium Carbonate is critical in maintaining quality. A suitable quality of Ground calcium carbonate (GCC) can be used in a number of coating applications as an extender, and an agent to either reduce or enhance gloss. Essentially as an extender/spacer for titanium dioxide for coating additive to densify the product.
